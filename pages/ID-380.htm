What Are Computer Peripherals?

Think of computer peripherals as anything that connects to your computer system to add functionality for work or entertainment. Most computer peripherals connect over a USB or video display connection like HDMI or DVI. PC accessories might also connect wirelessly over a Bluetooth connection or Wi-Fi. Explore our extensive collection of peripheral devices, ranging from keyboards to printers.

How Can I Optimize For Productivity and Enhance My Gaming Experience with PC Peripherals?

By definition, PC peripherals encompass a wide variety of computer accessories. These can be input/output devices like keyboards and mice, computer monitors, printers and scanners, and equipment related to power protection. Browse Newegg’s selection and discover how our peripheral devices can optimize your workspace for productivity and entertainment.

PC peripherals also encompass devices for media creation and video conferencing, such as webcams, microphones, headsets and speakers, and graphics tablets for drawing and animation. Upgrade your gaming experience with high-performance PC peripherals designed for immersive gameplay and precision.

Portable versus Stationary: Which Peripheral Devices Are Best Suited for Your Setup?

If you are looking for desktop peripherals to enhance your stationary setup, browse Newegg’s range of monitors, speakers, and more. While you can find portable computer peripherals you can carry with a laptop computer, most tend to stay in one place as part of a desktop setup. However, for greater mobility and flexibility in work environments, a USB docking station is a valuable tool for connecting multiple computer peripherals to a laptop computer using one convenient input. Docking stations can support up to three high-resolution monitors, plus an array of peripherals that connect over USB, providing laptop PC users with a desktop PC experience when they need it.

How Can I Use the Same Equipment with Multiple PCs Using Peripheral Devices?

Learn how to streamline your setup and maximize efficiency by using peripheral devices with multiple PCs. Users who want to use their computer peripherals with more than one computer will find a KVM switch to be useful. KVM stands for keyboard, video monitor, and mouse, and a KVM switch connects these devices into a single console that facilitates switching between computers as needed. This versatile solution will help enable seamless connectivity across different devices, optimizing your workflow and enhancing convenience.
